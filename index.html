The examples below show one potential solution for how to provide universal access to roads or paths in a minimally disruptive way. Some minimum access width is an important condition for providing important urban services like toilets, improved drainage, and emergency services.

Harare, Zimbabwe

In this example, we see a small block that has seven interior parcels (orange). These parcels do not have road access. By adding six road segements, just over 100m of new paths, we can connect all of the parcels to the road network.

Cape Town, South Africa

This example is a much larger block with 390 isolated parcels. To access some of the interior parcels, one must cross through at least five other parcels. The reblocking algorithm here adds over 1000m of paths to connect all the parcels.
